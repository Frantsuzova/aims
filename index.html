About AIMS

Explore the prospects of integrating digital technologies into the museum space at our international AIMs seminar.

While focused on innovation and digital transformation in museums, the seminar is dedicated to scenography, storytelling, and visitor experience. Our goal is to explore how new tools reshape exhibitions and perception.

The seminar is held in two locations: Belgrade (Serbia) and Saint Petersburg (Russia).

Main themes

Scenography

issues and methods for developing exhibition visual representation concepts; working with lighting and spatial composition; color theory within exhibition design tasks.

Storytelling

selecting narrative language; working with exhibits; curator and designer communication; challenges of effective information transfer.

Interactive Journey

integration of interactive elements into exhibitions (AR, VR); creating routes and scenarios to engage visitors; developing interactive installations; creating visual and sound effects.

Keywords: digital scenography, interfaces, augmented reality, virtual reality, machine learning, video projections, installations, audio design, interactivity, personalization of the visitor experience|
  • Video
    use of video as a narrative and spatial tool; designing projections that connect curatorial concepts with visitors’ sensory experience.
  • Audio Design
    creation of audio guides, sound installations, and interactive audio elements.
  • Interactive Interfaces
    development and implementation of applications and interactive panels that provide new ways for visitors to interact with museum content.
  • Data Analysis, Machine Learning and Visitor Experience
    applying data and artificial intelligence to personalize visits. Research results focused on analyzing the perception and impact of scenography's visual elements on visitors, as well as the effectiveness of routes and scenarios.
